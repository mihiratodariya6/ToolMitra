How Roman Numerals Work

The Roman numeral system originated in ancient Rome and remained the usual way of writing numbers throughout Europe well into the Late Middle Ages. Numbers in this system are represented by combinations of letters from the Latin alphabet.

The Basic Rules of Conversion

  • When a smaller symbol appears after a larger symbol, it is added (e.g., VI = 5 + 1 = 6).
  • When a smaller symbol appears before a larger symbol, it is subtracted (e.g., IX = 10 - 1 = 9).
  • The symbols I, X, C, and M can be repeated up to three times in succession. V, L, and D are never repeated.
